Privacy
Policy.

Locke & Boole Solutions LLC takes the privacy of client information and personal data with the same seriousness we apply to our investigative work. This policy explains what we collect, why, and how we protect it.

This Privacy Policy describes how Locke & Boole Solutions LLC ("the Firm," "we," "us") collects, uses, and protects personal information submitted through our website, intake forms, and client communications. By using this website or submitting an intake inquiry, you consent to the practices described in this policy.
Section 01

Overview

We operate a boutique intelligence firm. We are acutely aware that the information entrusted to us — about clients, about subjects of investigation, and about third parties — requires careful handling. Our data practices are designed with this in mind.

We collect only the minimum personal information necessary to operate our business. We do not sell personal data. We do not use client data for advertising. We do not share client identities or matter details with any third party except as required by law.

We do not sell, rent, license, or otherwise transfer personal information to third parties for marketing, advertising, or commercial purposes. This is unconditional.
Section 02

Information We Collect

Information you provide directly: When you submit an intake inquiry, sign a retainer agreement, or communicate with us, we collect the information you choose to provide — including your name, organization, email address, phone number, and matter details.

Newsletter subscriptions: If you subscribe to our newsletter or briefing distribution list, we collect your email address and, if provided, your name and firm affiliation.

Website usage data: Our website may collect standard server log data, including IP addresses, browser type, pages visited, and referral URLs. This data is used for security monitoring and aggregate traffic analysis only.

Communication records: We retain records of communications with clients and prospective clients for operational continuity and legal compliance purposes.

We do not collect payment card information directly. All payment processing is handled by third-party processors who maintain their own PCI-compliant data security standards.

Section 03

How We Use Information

We use the information we collect exclusively for the following purposes:

  • Evaluating and responding to client intake inquiries
  • Executing signed engagement letters and retainer agreements
  • Delivering Deliverables and ongoing monitoring reports to clients
  • Sending newsletter content and briefings to subscribers who have opted in
  • Maintaining internal records required for business operations and legal compliance
  • Improving the security and functionality of our website and communications infrastructure

We do not use personal information to build advertising profiles, conduct behavioral targeting, or enable any third-party marketing activities.

Section 04

Information Sharing

We do not sell, share, or disclose personal information to third parties except in the following limited circumstances:

  • Legal obligation: When required by valid legal process, including subpoenas, court orders, or regulatory demands. We will, where legally permissible, notify affected clients before complying.
  • Service providers: Limited technical service providers (such as encrypted email infrastructure) who are contractually bound to confidentiality and prohibited from using data for any purpose other than providing the specific service.
  • Successors: In the event of a merger, acquisition, or dissolution of the Firm, provided the successor entity is bound by equivalent privacy commitments.

In no case do we share client identities, matter details, or Deliverable contents with any party not directly involved in delivering our services to that client.

Section 05

Data Security

We implement industry-standard security practices to protect the information we hold, including:

  • End-to-end encrypted email communications (PGP encryption available upon request)
  • Encrypted storage for all client files and Deliverables
  • Strict internal access controls — only analysts directly assigned to a matter access its files
  • Secure, password-protected PDF delivery for all Deliverables
  • No use of personal client data on third-party cloud storage platforms without explicit consent

No data security system is impenetrable. If we become aware of a security incident affecting client data, we will notify affected clients promptly and take all reasonable steps to mitigate harm.

Section 06

Data Retention

We retain client data for the minimum period necessary to fulfill our contractual obligations and comply with applicable legal requirements. Generally:

  • Active engagement files are retained for the duration of the engagement plus 7 years, to satisfy standard professional recordkeeping requirements.
  • Intake inquiries that did not result in an engagement are retained for 12 months and then securely deleted.
  • Newsletter subscriber data is retained until the subscriber unsubscribes, at which point it is permanently deleted within 30 days.
  • Website server logs are retained for 90 days for security monitoring purposes.
Section 07

Your Rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have rights with respect to personal information we hold about you, including the right to access, correct, or request deletion of your data. To exercise any of these rights, contact us at contact@lockeandboole.com.

We will respond to verifiable requests within 30 days. We may need to verify your identity before processing certain requests. Some data may be exempt from deletion requests where retention is required by law or necessary to fulfill existing contractual obligations.

Newsletter subscribers may unsubscribe at any time using the unsubscribe link in any email or by contacting us directly.

Section 08

Cookies & Tracking

Our website uses minimal cookies strictly necessary for basic site functionality. We do not use advertising cookies, behavioral tracking pixels, or third-party analytics services that profile individual visitors.

We do not use Google Analytics, Facebook Pixel, or similar behavioral tracking tools. Any analytics we conduct are aggregate and non-identifiable.

Section 09

Children's Privacy

Our services are intended exclusively for legal professionals, corporate entities, and adult individuals engaged in lawful business activities. We do not knowingly collect personal information from individuals under the age of 18. If we become aware that we have inadvertently collected such information, we will delete it promptly.

Section 10

Policy Changes

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or applicable law. Material changes will be communicated to active clients via email. The date of the most recent revision is noted at the top of this policy.

Continued use of our website or services following a policy update constitutes acceptance of the revised terms.
