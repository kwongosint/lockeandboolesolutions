Methodology

Every finding
independently verifiable.

We operate on a strict data-first philosophy. We do not rely on proprietary black-box databases. We rely on the public record — and we cite every source.

Our Framework

The five-stage
investigation process.



Locke & Boole's methodology was developed over years of applied OSINT practice in legal, corporate, and compliance contexts. It is not proprietary in the sense of being hidden — every technique we use is available to any trained analyst. Our edge is execution speed, depth of cross-referencing, and institutional familiarity with the databases that matter.

We document everything. Every stage of an investigation is logged, sourced, and preserved in a format that can be independently audited — by opposing counsel, by a judge, or by your own internal team.

01
Entity Identification

We begin with Secretary of State filings, registered agent records, and annual reports across all jurisdictions relevant to the matter. This constructs the initial corporate family tree: all known legal entities connected to the target, their status, formation dates, and principal addresses.

Secretary of State portals Registered Agent Records Annual Reports
02
Financial Threading

EDGAR filings, federal procurement registries, and bankruptcy dockets are cross-referenced against UCC financing statements to surface financial interdependencies between entities — secured creditors, guarantors, and hidden encumbrances on assets.

EDGAR UCC Filings Federal Procurement Registry PACER / Bankruptcy Dockets
03
Physical Asset Verification

County tax assessor databases and real property records link entities to physical addresses and confirm the actual existence of claimed assets. Geospatial verification — satellite imagery and public mapping tools — provides independent confirmation that assets exist where and as described.

County Tax Assessor Databases Real Property Records Geospatial / Satellite Imagery
04
Ownership Chain Synthesis

All data threads are synthesized into a visual ownership chain — a "corporate family tree" that maps relationships between entities, natural persons, assets, and jurisdictions. Every node in the chart carries a cited source. The deliverable is formatted for immediate use in litigation or regulatory submissions.

All Prior Sources Visual Asset Genealogy Footnoted Citations
05
Continuous Monitoring (Retainer Clients)

For subscription clients, the investigation does not end at delivery. We maintain automated surveillance on the monitored entity set and generate alerts the moment material changes appear in the public record — registered agent substitutions, UCC amendments, entity dissolutions, or new encumbrances.

Automated Registry Monitoring UCC Amendment Tracking Entity Status Alerts
Primary Sources

The public record
contains the truth.

State & Federal Filings
  • Secretary of State business entity portals (all 50 states)
  • Registered agent and annual report records
  • UCC-1 financing statements and amendments
  • State-level franchise tax records
  • Assumed name / DBA filings
Federal Financial Data
  • SEC EDGAR — 10-K, S-1, 8-K, and beneficial ownership filings
  • Federal procurement and contractor award databases
  • PACER — Federal bankruptcy and civil dockets
  • FinCEN beneficial ownership registry (where accessible)
  • IRS Form 990 (nonprofit disclosures)
Real Property & Assets
  • County tax assessor and property appraiser databases
  • Grantor-grantee deed records by county
  • Mortgage and lien filings at county clerk level
  • State real property transfer records
  • Satellite and public mapping imagery for verification
OSINT & Open Record
  • State and federal FOIA request responses
  • Court dockets — state civil, probate, and family divisions
  • Offshore registry data (where publicly accessible)
  • Trade publication and business registry cross-references
  • Domain and WHOIS registration records
